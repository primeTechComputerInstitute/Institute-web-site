What is Computer Hardware and Networking?

Computer hardware refers to the physical components of a computer system that enable it to process, store, and manage data. These include the Central Processing Unit (CPU) for executing instructions, Random Access Memory (RAM) for temporary data storage, storage devices like hard drives (HDDs) and solid-state drives (SSDs) for long-term data retention, input devices such as keyboards and mice, output devices like monitors and printers, and the motherboard, which connects all components. Hardware also encompasses power supply units and cooling systems to ensure operational stability.

Computer networking refers to interconnecting computing devices like computers, servers, routers, switches, and network interface controllers (NICs) to facilitate data communication and resource sharing across networks. The main types of computer networks are local area networks (LANs), such as wireless LANs (WLANs), covering small areas like homes or offices; metropolitan area networks (MANs), spanning larger areas like cities; and wide area networks (WANs), covering expansive regions like countries or continents.
